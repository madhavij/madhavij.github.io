Extracting To and From Location from a sentence

Posted by Madhavi Jouhari on Mo. 19 Dezember 2016

While working on a usecase where location words had to be extracted from a sentence. Classifying what the source and destination places were, was one of the key requirements. This little snippet solved just one of the scenarios, which was achieved using regular expressions. To cover wider scenarios of extraction, use advanced natural language processing techniques for better results.

Task:

Get To and From location from a sentence like this:

I travelled from Stockholm to Antwerp on Wednesday.

Solution:

Step 1: Getting Source city

$ import re
$ sample = "I travelled from Stockholm to Antwerp in my car."
$ pattern = "from (.*) to"
$ fromLocation = re.compile(pattern)
$ fromLocation.search(sample)
$ _.group(1)
>>>Stockholm

Step 2: Getting Destination city

$ pattern2 = "to (\w+)"
$ toLocation = re.compile(pattern2)
$ toLocation.search(sample)
$ _.group(1)
>>>Antwerp

Ta da!

What is wrong with this?

This is not the cleanest of solutions, as it does not cover all the usecases.

For example : It fails to capture cases when city name is divided by spaces (like Los Angeles, New York).

Improvements

Still working to create a pattern that covers wider scenarios. This post was written just for this particular sentence structure. Please let me know if you have a better regex pattern to solve this.
